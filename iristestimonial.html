Iris Shen Academic Decathlon has taught me to pay more attention to detail, write more concisely, and work with others to achieve both personal and team goals. The experience is not purely "academic" as the name suggests. It builds social skills used in interacting with others that will become more important in life than just knowing stuff. It builds an identity when you find a balance between being an actively involved human being and an academically inclined individual. It deals with the value of team standards and the importance goal setting, preparation, grit, and carrying your weight. It deals with handling success with humility and defeat with grace. Most of all, it expands your worldview, and everyone's unique interests and backgrounds contribute very much to the environment.

Academic Decathlon is not about how smart you are, only how hard you work. Most importantly, it has taught me to to be open to new experiences and to appreciate the process of learning.
