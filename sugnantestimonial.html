Sugnan Suresh I competed in the Varsity GPA category as I did relatively poorly in my classes through high school, but decathlon was the place where I could work to prove to myself I am better than what my GPA shows. It was a place to commit to a goal with a team and created a safe environment to learn and study external to the pressures of school. By being able to commit to learning and studying by choice, in comparison to what was put on me in high school, I was able to focus my energy to succeed.

To me, the incentives for decathlon were more than just the cash prizes or medals; it was the camaraderie, the place to prove oneself, and the ability to show the world that my GPA wasn’t something to hold me back. Academic Decathlon showed me that my classes in high school weren’t the only factor in the understanding of my personal capabilities.
