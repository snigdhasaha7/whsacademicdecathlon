Kevin Li Academic Decathlon taught me how to read more critically, write more clearly, and think more rigorously about a wide range of disciplines. It taught me the value of grit, as my team and I fought together to achieve our common goals despite the different challenges that each event threw at us. But most important, it taught me to always love learning and to be open to new experiences - no matter how scary or difficult they seem.

I loved all the academic teams I had the privilege of being part of at Woodbridge. Decathlon was always my not-so-secret favorite, though. I’ve kept coming back to meetings even after I graduated and even supported the competition as a judge because I think there’s something genuinely special about it. Here are just a few of my favorite memories:

 - All the random inside jokes about the topics. Several of which somehow ended up on t-shirts.

 - The abundance of alpaca finger puppets and plushies.

 - Learning art history facts that still come in handy every time I visit a museum.

 - Helping teammates go from being shy at practice to winning medals in speech and interview. (Me included!)

 - Sitting on the edge of my seat cheering for Woodbridge in the Super Quiz.

 - Winning a bunch of medals at county and traveling to Sacramento!
