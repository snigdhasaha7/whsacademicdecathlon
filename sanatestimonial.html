Sana Datar My name is Sana and I was on the 2014-2015 Woodbridge Academic Decathlon team in the scholastic category. Being a part of Decathlon helped me build skills that were useful in both the short and long term. I learned how to stay organized, manage my schoolwork along with other responsibilities, and work as a team with other Decathlon students. I especially benefitted from the speech, interview, and essay sections in which I won medals at both county and state competitions. Not only was this a great addition to my college applications and resume, but I am still able to use these skills in various aspects of my life today.

I graduated with a B.S. in Health Sciences from Chapman University and I am currently pursuing my MPH (master’s in public health) at George Washington University. I built a strong foundation of skills during my time as a decathlete, and I have applied them through my work in the educational, legal, and medical fields, as well as in my schoolwork.

Finally, Decathlon allowed me to make friendships and networking connections that I’m sure will last for years to come.
